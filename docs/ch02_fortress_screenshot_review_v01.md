## Review Result

### 1. Fortress Ground Family Is Coherent

The frame now proves that fortress is more than a single tile experiment.

The two-tile family:

- reads as engineered ground
- contrasts cleanly against the established forest family
- remains subordinate to the units

### 2. Interaction Families Separate Correctly

The mixed object set is strong enough to justify the current family split:

- altar reads sacred
- lever reads local mechanical interaction
- gate control reads broader route/system machinery

That is enough to support continued chapter-specific environment work without collapsing all interactables into one category.

### 3. Character Distance Holds On Fortress Ground

The frontline/heavy/hostile spacing still works over the fortress floor language.

This matters because:

- fortress-like spaces are more geometric and darker than forest
- that could have caused silhouette flattening
- the current preview indicates the roster still wins the read

## What This Pass Approves

Approved for continued use:

- `fortress_tile_01` as a map-specific fortified-ground family anchor
- `fortress_tile_02` as the second family variant
- `altar_01`, `lever_01`, and `gate_control_01` as distinct interaction families in fortified spaces

## What This Pass Does Not Approve

Not approved yet:

- promoting fortress to a global terrain baseline
- treating the preview composition as final art sign-off
- assuming no additional fortress-family support assets are needed

## Working Conclusion

The CH02 fortress screenshot assembly pass is successful.

That means:

- fortress-family work can continue as real production work
- chapter-specific fortified maps now have a validated art language
- future work should expand the family, not redesign it

## Best Next Step

The highest-value follow-up is:

- one CH06-oriented fortified preview pass

Reason:

- CH02 proves the family in an early fortress lane
- CH06 would prove the same family under a harsher siege/iron-keep identity
