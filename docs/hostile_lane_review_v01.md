# Hostile Lane Review V01

## Purpose

This document records the first comparison pass for the hostile sprite lane after the project gained two enemy anchor classes:

- Enemy Raider
- Enemy Skirmisher

It exists to lock what is already working and to prevent future enemy work from collapsing back into one generic hostile body type.

## Current Hostile Anchor Set

### Enemy Raider

Working role:

- baseline hostile melee infantry
- line pressure
- rigid, suppressive field presence

Current selected set:

- idle `v01`
- move `v01`
- attack `v01`

### Enemy Skirmisher

Working role:

- lighter hostile pursuit unit
- faster pressure lane
- second enemy class-distance proof

Current selected set:

- idle `v01`
- move `v01`
- attack `v01`

## What Is Working

### 1. Hostile Family Cohesion Exists

The two enemy anchors already feel related.

Shared traits that are now stable:

- more compressed posture than allies
- harsher body language
- controlled ember-red hostility cue
- cleaner authority-driven gear language than outlaw/bandit fantasy

### 2. Raider And Skirmisher Do Not Collapse Into One Lane

The current distance is good enough for production:

- Raider reads heavier and more suppressive
- Skirmisher reads lighter and more pursuit-oriented

That means the enemy side has passed the minimum “one hostile silhouette family only” trap.

### 3. Enemy Units Still Belong To The Same Game As The Ally Roster

This is important.

The enemy lane is hostile, but it has not drifted into:

- western grimdark realism
- monster-poster roughness
- assassin fantasy glamour

It still belongs to the same tactical JRPG rendering family as the allies.

## What Is Not Fully Solved Yet

### 1. Hostile Lane Still Needs More Breadth

Two enemy anchors are enough to define direction, but not enough to cover the whole enemy faction.

Missing future lanes include:

- enemy heavy unit
- enemy caster or ritual unit
- late-game authority elite

### 2. Skirmisher Still Needs Runtime Parity In Broader Preview Surfaces

It has source sheets and runtime frames, but it has not yet been propagated through as many review surfaces as the core ally anchors.

### 3. Enemy Accent Discipline Must Stay Tight

Ember-red works now because it is restrained.

If future hostile designs spread red too broadly, the lane will become noisy and cheap.

## Locked Conclusions

These conclusions are now stable enough to use:

- hostility should read through posture and silhouette before accent color
- raider-family enemies are pressure blocks
- skirmisher-family enemies are pursuit pressure
- enemy lanes should stay in the same rendering family as allies
- “more hostile” must not mean “more realistic” or “more chaotic”

## Failure Conditions

Reject future hostile sheets if:

- the unit reads like an ally recolor
- the red accent does all the work
- the skirmisher becomes too close to Tia
- the raider becomes too close to Bran
- the finish drifts into gritty western or monster-poster art

## Recommended Next Hostile Work

Highest-value next addition:

1. enemy heavy unit

Why:

- it completes the hostile pressure ladder
- it creates a real enemy analogue to Bran without copying Bran
- it sharpens ally/heavy vs enemy/heavy separation

Second-highest:

2. enemy caster or ritual lane

Why:

- it will test whether hostile magic can stay distinct from Serin-like support language

## Working Result

The hostile lane is now stable enough to support continued production.

It should be treated as:

- `direction lock: yes`
- `full faction coverage: no`
- `runtime-ready baseline: yes`
