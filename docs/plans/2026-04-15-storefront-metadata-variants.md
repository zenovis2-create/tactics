## Long Description Variants

### Long Description A — Design-led (Steam audience)

**Ashen Bell** is a single-player tactics RPG built around a question most games avoid:

*What if forgetting was the real enemy?*

---

**Memory as Mechanic**

In Ashen Bell, the protagonist Rian has no past — only instinct. As you fight across ten chapters of grid-based tactical combat, memory fragments surface. Each one unlocks a new battlefield command. Each one also reveals something uncomfortable about who Rian used to be.

The story is not a mystery you watch. It is a system you interact with.

---

**Forgetting Has Consequences**

The signature status effect, 망각 (Forgetting), stacks as enemies apply pressure. Accuracy, evasion, and skill access degrade. Push too far and Rian's signature abilities go dark. Your squad healer exists specifically to undo this — because in Ashen Bell, healing means helping an ally stay themselves.

---

**Trust Is a Tactical Variable**

Six companions. Six personal arcs. Bond level is not a cosmetic stat — adjacency with a trusted ally unlocks support attacks, damage sharing, and status resistance. The final battle's hardest mechanic can only be cleared if your bonds are real.

---

**A Game That Earns Its Ending**

There is a bad ending and a true ending. The difference is not a checklist. It is whether you played in a way that proves you understood what the game was asking.

---

*Mobile-first design. Turn-based tactical combat. Dark fantasy tone.*

---

### Long Description B — Narrative-led (mobile / App Store audience)

A soldier with no name. A war with no clean sides. A past that gets harder to face the more you remember.

**Ashen Bell** is a tactics RPG that asks: can you move forward while still remembering everything?

---

**Lead a squad across ten chapters of grid combat** — each battle shaped by terrain, positioning, and the weight of what your commander used to be.

**Recover memory fragments** as you play. Each one changes how you fight. Each one changes what you know about Rian.

**Build trust with six companions** — Serin the healer who fights to keep memories intact, Bran the knight who refuses to forget the lives he lost, Tia who demands accountability before she offers loyalty.

**Face bosses who believe in what they're doing.** The antagonists of Ashen Bell are not villains. They are people who found a way to make forgetting feel like mercy.

The harder question: were they wrong?

---

*Original IP. Turn-based tactical combat. Dark fantasy.*
