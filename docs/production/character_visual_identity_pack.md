# Character Visual Identity Pack

## Purpose

This document locks the in-game visual language for character-facing art in `잿빛의 기억`.
It covers the playable roster, enemy command silhouettes, portrait rules, battlefield readability, icon/effect grammar, and consistency guardrails that keep UI, environment, effects, portraits, and promotional support art speaking the same language.

This pack is for in-game production art.
It is not a store-key-art brief and it does not redefine story canon, systems, or character arcs.

## Direction Lock

### Chosen Direction

`Classic tactical JRPG portrait illustration`

- The target is closer to late-1990s to early-2000s console SRPG portrait grammar than to western dark-fantasy concept art.
- The emotional reference lane should feel adjacent to classic console tactics portraits such as `Farland Tactics`: warm low-fantasy, modest heroism, clean faces, and readable class identity.
- Faces should read clean, idealized, and expressive in a restrained anime-adjacent way.
- Rendering should stay soft and readable, with crisp line discipline and controlled cel-style shading.
- Tactical identity and party readability still win over decoration.

### Why This Direction

- The current western concept-sheet lane pushes the game too far away from the emotional reference point of classic tactics RPGs.
- Full painterly realism muddies both mobile readability and nostalgic tactical warmth.
- Full glossy modern anime rendering would break the game's subdued grief-heavy tone.
- A classic tactical JRPG lane keeps the project readable, character-forward, warm, and emotionally legible without turning it into collector-key-art.

## Visual Thesis

The game should feel like a world trying to preserve names inside ash, fog, records, and command marks.

Every surface should reinforce four constants:

1. `Readability first`
2. `Grief without melodrama`
3. `Memory represented as marks, records, maps, and fracture geometry`
4. `Authority and erasure represented through rigid order, seal forms, and controlled emptiness`

## Cross-Surface Constants

### Palette Families

- `Ash blue-gray`: default world base for stone, fog, iron, and military cloth.
- `Bone parchment`: records, codex paper, icon fills, and neutral panel fields.
- `Vow gold`: ally trust, sacred protection, selected-state emphasis, and noble trim.
- `Ember red`: hostile telegraphs, boss threat, execution lines, and warning accents.
- `Moss green`: ranger craft, forest survival, and reclaimed life.
- `Soft sky blue`: portrait highlight family for support characters, healing motifs, and clean high-value edge contrast.
- `Void black`: controlled use only for erasure zones, censorship marks, and late-game null read.

### Value Discipline

- Mid values dominate the environment.
- High-value contrast belongs to UI, telegraphs, holy protection, and focal portrait planes.
- Boss fights may briefly introduce stronger contrast spikes, but only around gameplay-relevant information.

### Shape Language

- Allies: readable asymmetry, anchored stances, practical gear, one memorable silhouette cut per unit.
- Empire/authority: verticals, wedges, seals, rigid symmetry, compressed posture.
- Memory artifacts: circles broken by grid lines, map ticks, torn paper edges, stamped marks, directional arrows.
- Oblivion/erasure: smooth subtraction, hollow centers, clipped names, missing wedges, sound-ring voids.

### Material Finish

- Cloth, leather, iron, parchment, stone, and aged wood should feel tactile but illustrated rather than hyper-rendered.
- Materials should be simplified into readable planes, not painted into gritty realism.
- Gold should read as ceremonial trim, not luxury ornament.
- Glow is restrained and gameplay-motivated.

## Portrait Rules

### Framing

- Default crop: chest-up or waist-up.
- Eyes must be readable first, gear second.
- Portrait angle should support role clarity:
  - commanders and knights face with weight and intent
  - support characters face with openness or stabilizing calm
  - assassins and archivists may use sharper side angles

### Expression

- Avoid exaggerated gacha-style emotion and avoid stoic western grimdark blankness.
- Emotion should be readable through eyes, brows, mouth corners, jaw set, and shoulder angle.
- The emotional register is `warm restraint`, not melodrama and not dead-eyed realism.

### Background Treatment

- Use restrained background fields with factional texture or chapter motif.
- Portrait backdrops should never compete with the face.
- Memory-bearing characters may include subtle map-line or record-fragment overlays behind them.

### Render Discipline

- Use clean linework with soft cel-shaded fills and restrained painterly blending.
- Skin planes should be simple and luminous, not heavily textured.
- Weapon edges, insignia, seals, and high-value facial planes stay crisp.
- Do not push faces into western realism or into glossy collectible-anime polish.
- Keep the finish slightly flatter and more print-like than modern live-service character art.
- Prefer gentle analog warmth over glossy digital sheen.

### Retro Tactical Mood Guardrails

- Portrait sheets should feel like in-game or guidebook tactical portraits, not like a modern concept-art lineup.
- Keep the composition simple: four-up portraits, clean spacing, neutral backplates, no bold title cards or decorative labels.
- Avoid cinematic rim-lighting, dramatic vignettes, poster framing, or premium-card presentation.
- Readability should come from silhouette, face, and color discipline, not from spectacle.

## Battlefield Readability Rules

### Unit Read

- Each unit needs one dominant silhouette read at zoomed-out scale.
- Weapons, cloaks, shoulder mass, banner shapes, braids, and support apparatus should be used to differentiate roles.
- Avoid silhouettes that rely only on color swaps.

### Team Separation

- Ally read uses warmer highlight accents, cleaner contours, and more open silhouette rhythm.
- Enemy read uses harsher angle logic, colder neutrals, and more compressed posture.
- Neutral or civilian read avoids both strong ally gold and hostile ember-red framing.

### Telegraphs And Effects

- Effects must explain rule outcomes, not decorate them.
- Move/target/danger states use layered edge logic:
  - low-opacity area fill
  - strong outer edge
  - icon or pattern reinforcement for critical states
- `Color alone is forbidden` for major tactical warnings.
- Memory waves should use map-ring, stamp, and fracture motifs.
- Oblivion effects should subtract or blur local identity, never become generic purple magic.

## UI And Icon Grammar

### UI Tone

- Panels feel like classic tactical command windows and preserved records, not luxury fantasy menus.
- Use clean plate shapes, readable borders, and light parchment-style contrast instead of muddy dark realism.
- Use bone, ash, iron, soft blue, and controlled gold as the base UI family.

### Icon Rules

- Icons should be simple enough to read at mobile scale.
- Every icon needs a clear central shape plus one secondary recognition detail.
- Use the memory grammar consistently:
  - record / evidence / codex: sheets, stamps, seal breaks, tabs
  - command / tactic: arrows, lane marks, cross-grid ticks
  - healing / protection: held ring, shield arc, stabilizing hand, lit vow mark
  - erasure / oblivion: clipped ring, severed name-bar, hollow seal

### Line Weight

- Keep icon line weight firm and even.
- Icons should feel closer to classic tactics-RPG menu art than to modern painterly inventory cards.
- Small FX symbols should look related to menu icons, not like separate concept-art brushwork.

## Environment And Character Coupling

- Characters should look born from the environment, not pasted onto it.
- Chapter color bias may shift, but character identity must survive local lighting.
- Environment motifs should echo the chapter's emotional job:
  - CH1: ash, pursuit lines, cold dawn, ruined stone, short bursts of protective gold
  - CH3/8 forest zones: moisture, dark greens, trap craft, fractured moonlight
  - CH4/7 faith and purification spaces: pale stone, thin water sheen, sanctified geometry under strain
  - CH5/9 archive spaces: parchment, soot, shelves, ink-black censorship, coded seals
  - CH10: monumental emptiness, broken name-anchors, law and oblivion colliding

## Boss Differentiation Rules

### General Boss Rule

- Each boss needs a silhouette that reads before detail.
- Bosses should feel like named tactical opponents, not western monster-poster concepts.
- Shape mass, posture, heraldry, and telegraph logic create presence first.
- Do not rely on oversized VFX to create presence.

### Roderick

- Rigid pursuit profile, sharp authority lines, disciplined red threat read.

### Basil

- Drowned priest structure, vertical ritual read, water-heavy sanctified decay.

### Saria

- Mercy-as-erasure read: elegance under tension, controlled sacred geometry, dangerous stillness.

### Valgar

- Fortress mass, artillery implication, immovable forward pressure.

### Lete

- Predatory negative space, fragmented read, vision-denial silhouette.

### Melchion

- Archive tyrant geometry, revised law through symbols, codex and seal authority over raw muscle.

### Karuon

- Human authority first, void kingship second.
- Early read is lawful command.
- Final read is a name-devouring absence built from broken regal geometry, not monster sludge.

## Promotional Consistency Guardrails

- Promotional support art may widen contrast and staging, but may not invent a second art style.
- Do not switch to western grimdark realism.
- Do not switch to high-saturation character-collector poster logic.
- Promo faces should still preserve tactical seriousness and low-fantasy warmth.
- UI motifs used in promo should come from the same record/map/seal vocabulary used in-game.

## Prompting Rules For Production Art

- Prompts should specify gameplay role, silhouette read, emotional restraint, material set, and chapter or faction motifs.
- Prompts should explicitly ask for `classic tactical JRPG portrait feel`, `clean linework`, `soft cel shading`, and `warm readable anime-proportioned faces`.
- Prompts should explicitly ask for `late-1990s console SRPG warmth`, `slightly flatter cel shading`, and `retro tactical portrait-sheet mood`.
- Prompts should forbid western dark-fantasy concept-sheet rendering, over-glow, glossy gacha rendering, and decorative particle spam.
- Prompts should forbid card-frame presentation, name labels baked into the art, modern splash-art polish, and heavy skin rendering.
- If a prompt asks for memory motifs, use record/map/stamp/fracture language rather than vague dream magic.
- Final canon decisions stay in writing; prompts inherit those decisions, they do not make them.

## Non-Negotiable Rejects

- western grimdark concept-art faces
- generic purple fantasy VFX
- glossy gacha face rendering
- over-ornamented holy designs that erase field practicality
- unreadable dark-on-dark silhouettes
- boss presence created only by oversized particle fog
- UI icons that look unrelated to battlefield FX or codex motifs
