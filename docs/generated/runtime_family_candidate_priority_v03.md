# Runtime Family Candidate Priority V03

## Purpose

This document refreshes the runtime family candidate order after six chapter-local families are live.

Live families today:

- `well`
- `battery`
- `shrine`
- `floodgate`
- `evidence`
- `bell`

This document only ranks what should come next.

## Wave 2 Candidates

### 1. `anchor_chain_01`

Recommended role:

- `chain_control`

Why first:

- already tied to a concrete CH10 gameplay contract
- clearly distinct from `gate_control`
- strong terminal-object silhouette
- directly connected to a late-game objective-state shift

Risk:

- tightly coupled to endgame logic, so it should stay narrow and stage-specific at first

### 2. `archive_lectern_01`

Recommended role:

- `keeper_lectern`

Why second:

- already tied to a concrete CH09B gameplay contract
- separates cleanly from `truth_dais_01`
- high-value late-game control surface with clear stage meaning

Risk:

- may overlap with `evidence` if the role is not kept strictly “keeper control” rather than “truth-bearing object”

### 3. `split_marker_post_01`

Recommended role:

- `route_marker`

Why third:

- useful route-reading silhouette
- clearly distinct from altar-like objects
- chapter-local pressure meaning is stable

Risk:

- route markers become repetitive faster than stronger landmark silhouettes
- may benefit from a `V02` variant before wiring widely

### 4. `transfer_gate_latch_01`

Recommended role:

- `latch`

Why fourth:

- strong CH08-specific route-release meaning
- already tied to a gameplay contract

Risk:

- overlaps with `lever` and `gate_control` more than the candidates above

### 5. `city_seal_dais_01`

Recommended role:

- `civic_seal`

Why fifth:

- strong chapter identity
- public authority read is stable

Risk:

- overlaps with `altar` and `evidence` if introduced too early

## Working Conclusion

The project has moved past first-wave family creation.

Wave 2 should favor:

1. the clearest remaining gameplay contracts
2. late-stage objects that already exist in real stage logic
3. the lowest overlap with the six live families

By that standard, `anchor_chain_01` is now the best next runtime family candidate.
