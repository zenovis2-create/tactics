## Comparison Goal

The family should read in one glance as:

- sword: straight service blade
- shield: broad defense mass
- staff: compact sacred support tool
- bow: agile ranged arc
- lance: long thrusting military line
- heavy armor: broad chest-and-shoulder defense mass
- robe: calm vertical support silhouette

If any lane starts borrowing another lane's silhouette hook, it should be corrected before more variants are added.

## Current Read Check

### Sword

- First read: disciplined one-handed field weapon
- Must stay distinct from: lance and staff
- Drift risk: becoming a generic silver line with no guard break

### Shield

- First read: defense-first heavy class identifier
- Must stay distinct from: armor slab or wall prop
- Drift risk: becoming too ornate or too flat

### Staff

- First read: calm sacred support equipment
- Must stay distinct from: lance and relic wand
- Drift risk: head motif shrinking or magic accent overpowering structure

### Bow

- First read: compact ranged field weapon
- Must stay distinct from: decorative branch or oversized heroic longbow
- Drift risk: grip break disappearing or arc becoming too thin

### Lance

- First read: disciplined thrust weapon for officer/cavalry lanes
- Must stay distinct from: staff and halberd
- Drift risk: head becoming too small or silhouette becoming a plain stick

### Heavy Armor

- First read: broad allied heavy-defense equipment
- Must stay distinct from: shield slab or statue fragment
- Drift risk: chest silhouette flattening into a rectangle or detail noise overtaking the mass

### Robe

- First read: calm support-class garment
- Must stay distinct from: cape, banner, or luxury dress
- Drift risk: vertical silhouette collapsing into a plain cloth block or trim becoming louder than the form
