## Comparison Goal

The player should be able to read these objects before UI help as:

- battery emplacement: siege-pressure landmark
- banner mast: fortress identity and recapture landmark
- lever: local mechanism
- gate control: route or system control
- altar: sacred objective

If CH02 landmarks collapse into lever or gate-control language, chapter-local clarity has failed.

## Current Read Check

### Battery Emplacement

- First read: artillery pressure and aimed threat
- Must stay distinct from: gate control and cart-like machinery
- Drift risk: reading like a local device instead of a major siege landmark

### Banner Mast

- First read: fortress identity and rally landmark
- Must stay distinct from: decorative flag clutter and altar-like focal props

### Lever

- First read: local mechanism

- Drift risk: becoming oversized enough to impersonate route-control or artillery language

### Gate Control

- First read: route and system control

### Altar

- First read: sacred objective

- Drift risk: being used as generic dramatic focal prop in military scenes

## Current Conclusion

CH02 now has a usable chapter-local landmark split:

- `battery_emplacement_01` for siege pressure and danger direction
- `banner_mast_01` for fortress identity and recapture tone

This allows lever, gate control, and altar to keep their broader systemic meanings instead of doing all fortress reading work alone.
