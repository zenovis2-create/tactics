# Landmark Runtime Promotion Priority V01

## Purpose

This document defines the next promotion order for chapter-local landmark assets.

It answers one question:

`Which chapter-specific landmarks should move from support-only artifacts into stronger runtime object-icon or board-facing roles first?`

Promotion here means:

- the landmark is no longer only a review artifact
- it becomes the preferred source for a real in-game presentation surface
- future polish builds on it instead of replacing the whole direction

## Promotion Criteria

Promote first when an asset is:

1. already clean and runtime-derived
2. highly legible at small size
3. tied to a strong chapter mechanic or objective
4. unlikely to drift soon

## Promote First

### 1. `memory_well_01`

Why first:

- CH01 investigation landmark is already cleanly separated from altar and cache
- the well is a stronger unique silhouette than most small props
- it supports both chapter identity and interaction readability

Promotion target:

- chapter object icon
- investigation support card

### 2. `battery_emplacement_01`

Why now:

- CH02 siege-pressure language depends on the battery reading immediately
- its silhouette is already strong at reduced size
- it is one of the clearest chapter-defining military landmarks

Promotion target:

- chapter object icon
- battlefield pressure support surface

### 3. `resin_shrine_01`

Why now:

- CH03 needs one chapter-local object that is not just altar reuse
- it already separates hidden ritual from generic sacred-object language
- it is more unique than generic forest clutter and therefore worth preserving

Promotion target:

- chapter object icon
- hidden-landmark support card

### 4. `floodgate_wheel_01`

Why now:

- CH04 water-control is mechanic-critical
- the wheel silhouette reads quickly and maps cleanly to an icon
- it separates route-state machinery from altar and purification surfaces

Promotion target:

- chapter object icon
- route-state support surface

### 5. `truth_dais_01`

Why now:

- CH05 archive pressure depends on one trustworthy evidence anchor
- the dais reads more cleanly than most archive clutter
- it is a likely candidate for dossier or records-facing support surfaces later

Promotion target:

- chapter object icon
- evidence or truth support surface

## Promote Second

### 6. `shield_wreck_01`

Why second:

- excellent chapter-local cover read
- slightly less universal as an object-icon than the first tier because it competes with generic cover clutter

### 7. `bell_frame_01`

Why second:

- strong CH07 symbol
- but likely to want one more round of civic/ritual contrast review before promotion

### 8. `split_marker_post_01`

Why second:

- useful chapter-local route cue
- but route cues can become repetitive faster than anchor landmarks and may want a `V02` sooner

### 9. `archive_lectern_01`

Why second:

- clear role in CH09B
- but root-archive late-game pressure may still evolve and could reshape the preferred presentation surface

### 10. `anchor_chain_01`

Why second:

- highly important in CH10
- but it is tightly coupled to late-game rule messaging and may benefit from waiting until terminal UI surfaces are more defined

## Hold For Later

These should stay support-only for now unless a specific UI or runtime slot appears:

- `scavenged_cache_01`
- `evac_handcart_01`
- `banner_mast_01`
- `hunter_rig_01`
- `purification_basin_01`
- `seal_frame_01`
- `chain_lift_winch_01`
- `city_seal_dais_01`
- `transfer_gate_latch_01`
- `revision_core_01`
- `bell_dais_01`

Reason:

- these are valuable and valid,
- but they either
  - serve narrower chapter roles,
  - may want a second variant later,
  - or are stronger as board/support assets than as first-wave object icons.

## Working Conclusion

The best first-wave runtime promotion queue for chapter-local landmarks is:

1. `memory_well_01`
2. `battery_emplacement_01`
3. `resin_shrine_01`
4. `floodgate_wheel_01`
5. `truth_dais_01`

That wave gives the project:

- early chapter diversity
- one strong representative landmark for multiple chapter grammars
- low-risk icon-facing candidates

without overcommitting to weaker or more context-dependent props.
