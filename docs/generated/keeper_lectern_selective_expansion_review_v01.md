# Keeper Lectern Selective Expansion Review V01

## Purpose

This document reviews whether `keeper_lectern` should receive a second
records-facing surface in `selective expansion pass 2`.

It does not assume the expansion is already approved.

It tests whether the expansion would stay cleaner than `evidence`.

## Current State

`keeper_lectern` is already:

- a live runtime family
- wired to `CH09B_05`
- distinct from `evidence` at runtime routing level

Current safe reading:

- keeper-mediated archive handling
- controlled access
- guided archive navigation

## Candidate Expansion Direction

Allowed direction under review:

- one additional records-facing or codex-facing support surface

Not under review:

- briefing-first usage
- generalized archive-family expansion
- multi-stage rollout by default

## Expansion Test

Approve the next surface only if all three stay true:

1. it reads as keeper mediation, not truth-bearing proof
2. it improves archive comprehension, not just archive atmosphere
3. it does not force `evidence` and `keeper_lectern` into the same panel role

## Best Candidate Surface

The best allowed destination is:

- a records-side support surface adjacent to existing `evidence` entries

Why:

- it matches the current family strength
- it does not force a briefing role
- it keeps the distinction between archive authority and archive handling

## Main Risk

The main risk is role collapse.

That happens if a new surface makes `keeper_lectern` read as:

- proof
- witness authority
- archive seal
- discovered truth

Those belong to `evidence`, not to `keeper_lectern`.

## Current Decision

Decision for pass 2:

- `keeper_lectern` may receive one additional records-facing support surface
- but only if the surface role is explicitly “archive handling” or “keeper guidance”

## Working Conclusion

`keeper_lectern` is the cleanest family to review first in pass 2 because it
has real records-facing value without needing to become a first-wave dossier
family.
