## Wave 3 Candidates

### 1. `archive_lectern_01`

Recommended role:

- `keeper_lectern`

Why first:

- late-game archive control still has one clean remaining contract that is not
  fully covered by `evidence`
- the object reads more like keeper-side guidance or control furniture than a
  truth-bearing witness point
- it can expand CH09B without collapsing back into generic sacred-object logic

Risk:

- if the role drifts toward truth-bearing archive authority, it will overlap
  with `evidence`

Constraint:

- keep it narrowly scoped to keeper mediation, archive handling, or guided
  revision access

### 2. `split_marker_post_01`

Recommended role:

- `route_marker`

Why second:

- route commitment is still a readable contract not yet covered by live
  families
- the silhouette is simple enough to survive small icon treatment
- it offers a planning-facing or navigation-facing category rather than another
  sacred or machine family

Risk:

- route markers become repetitive quickly
- may need a stronger pairing rule or variant strategy before broad rollout

Constraint:

- keep it tied to divided-route guidance, not generic signpost use

### 3. `transfer_gate_latch_01`

Recommended role:

- `latch`

Why third:

- CH08 has a real route-release contract that is narrower than `gate_control`
- the family could express blocked-lane relief or release-state interaction
  without inheriting full machinery semantics

Risk:

- overlaps with `lever` and `gate_control` more than the candidates above
- easy to turn into a mere alternate icon unless its wording stays precise

Constraint:

- keep it focused on release-state gating, not all local switches

### 4. `city_seal_dais_01`

Recommended role:

- `civic_seal`

Why fourth:

- public authority and oath-state control are still distinct from current live
  families
- the object has stable chapter identity and stronger records-facing value than
  many remaining props

Risk:

- overlaps with `bell` on civic pressure
- overlaps with `evidence` on authority-bearing ritual surfaces

Constraint:

- keep it tied to oath, seal, and civic legitimacy rather than generic city
  ritual

### 5. `bell_dais_01`

Recommended role:

- `final_toll_anchor`

Why fifth:

- it has strong endgame identity and could support CH10-specific terminal
  control or final-toll framing
- it remains visually distinct from `anchor_chain_01`

Risk:

- very high overlap with `bell`
- likely better treated as a stage-specific landmark before becoming a reusable
  family

Constraint:

- do not open it unless CH10 needs a second clearly distinct endgame object type

## Working Conclusion

The next family should not simply add more chapter flavor.

It should add a new readable contract that still survives:

1. battle marker use
2. stage-authored data
3. at least one non-battle UI surface

By that standard, `archive_lectern_01` is now the best next runtime family
candidate.
