## Test Priorities

### P0. Build integrity after art swap

- project still boots cleanly
- no missing texture, atlas, scene, or import dependency breaks Gate 0
- no art drop causes a crash, hang, invisible scene, or unreadable HUD state

### P1. Representative frame readability

- tutorial, CH03, CH07, and CH10 representative frames still read at a glance
- replacements do not reduce legibility of units, objects, terrain, HUD, or FX
- each chapter keeps enough silhouette and contrast separation to remain visually distinct in the contact sheet

### P2. Slice-path functional confidence

- core demo path still clears with `m1_playtest_runner.gd`
- HUD and camp shell snapshot surfaces still clear with `m3_ui_runner.gd`
- art changes do not hide objectives, state changes, turn flow, or interaction targets

## Manual Review Buckets

Every batch review must explicitly check these five buckets against the representative frames:

### Units

- player and enemy tokens remain identifiable without relying on hover or labels
- faction ownership is visible from color, value, silhouette, or framing
- selected, damaged, and acted states remain readable

### Objects

- levers, gates, chests, control points, and interactables remain distinguishable from non-interactive scenery
- object importance can be recognized before reading objective text

### Terrain

- walkable space, blocked space, hazards, and elevation cues remain visually separable
- replacement textures do not camouflage pathing lanes or objective routes

### HUD

- objective text, turn state, and unit info remain legible over the updated art
- art contrast does not drown panel edges, portraits, icons, or status markers

### FX

- telegraphs, hits, marks, and objective triggers remain visible during normal battle pacing
- replacement FX do not obscure units, grid logic, or objective tiles

## Quality Gates

### Batch Entry Gate

An art replacement batch may enter review only if all of the following are true:

- changed files are limited to approved visual surfaces for the current slice
- the batch declares which of the five review buckets it touches
- the reviewer has the current representative snapshots and contact sheet available

### Patch Gate

A patch may move forward only if all of the following are true:

- Gate 0 passes after the asset drop
- the representative snapshot renderer completes successfully
- no new regression is found in units, objects, terrain, HUD, or FX
- any visible difference in tutorial, CH03, CH07, or CH10 is explainable as intended art replacement rather than accidental drift
- no changed frame makes gameplay state harder to read than the baseline

### Build Gate

A build or candidate patch may move forward only if all of the following are true:

- patch gate is already satisfied
- `m1_playtest_runner.gd` passes on the candidate
- `m3_ui_runner.gd` passes on the candidate
- the contact sheet still shows chapter-to-chapter mood separation instead of flattening into one visual tone
- any unresolved issue is documented as polish debt, not a readability regression

## Stop Conditions

The batch is blocked immediately if any of the following are true:

- missing imports, broken references, or runtime errors appear after the art drop
- a unit, object, objective, or telegraph can no longer be read reliably in the representative frames
- terrain treatment makes movement lanes, hazards, or interaction tiles ambiguous
- HUD text or state markers lose legibility because of contrast or visual noise
- the renderer or core runners stop passing on the candidate

## Evidence Required Before Move-Forward

Before a build or patch advances, all of these statements must be true:

- the four representative chapter frames were regenerated for the exact candidate under review
- the contact sheet was regenerated for the exact candidate under review
- Gate 0, M1 playtest, and M3 UI results are recorded as observed passes, not assumed passes
- any visual deltas were reviewed bucket-by-bucket for units, objects, terrain, HUD, and FX
- no reviewer is relying on asset intent alone; the candidate was judged from runtime output

## Decision Rule

The current vertical slice can tolerate concept-derived visuals and placeholder polish.
It cannot tolerate a production art batch that makes the slice less readable than the current baseline.
